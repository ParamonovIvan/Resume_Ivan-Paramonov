BREWING

In 2015 I tried craft beer for the first time and then spent 2 years tasting whatever styles I came across. As a result I've tastred more than 400 sorts, which entailed immersion in the craft beer environment and I finally have fallen in love with beer. I wanted to become more than just a taster. In 2017, I bought equipment, spent hours on beer forums, talked to brewers, and started brewing. Every day, after job, on any day off, I do what lights me up - I crush malt by hand, carry water from the spring, make experiments with hops and yeast, and pore over the kitchen stove in hope of getting the perfect drink. The total volume of my first brew was 40 liters, and it instantly was sold out, to friends and acquaintances. Then some beer geeks, owners of small craft beer bars and tasters began to take an interest in me. Because I challenge myself every time and don't choose just one style, I mastered about 350 styles, which amounted to 12 tons of finished products. My goal is to brew not just craft beer for sale, but to brew unusual, sometimes forgotten and unpopular varieties, to surprise with incompatible combinations of ingredients, and to write recipes. Invozho No. 2, February 2020

Section "Business"

Ivan Paramonov: builder, saxophonist and brewer

We begin the series of materials in honor of the year of entrepreneurship in Udmurtia with an introduction to an unusual startup. Ivan Paramonov from Izhevsk brews homemade beer. The guy is 28 years old, he was born in the city of Tchaikovsky, Perm Territory.

Builder

- How did you choose your profession and where did you study?
- I studied at the Tchaikovsky branch of Izhevsk State Technical University in the field of Construction. In 2015, he graduated from Izhevsk State Technical University with a master's degree in "Project Management in Construction." I submitted my documents to the university with the thought: “They built, are building and will continue to build.” So construction became my profession.

- During your student years, you were interested in tourism.
- It started at school. I attended tourism classes for 4 years: I tied knots with the guys, went into the forest for orienteering and an obstacle course, and spent the night in tents for 3 days. While studying in Izhevsk, I dreamed of going to the Chelyabinsk region. Gathered a group of 7 people. Thus happened an exciting seven-day backpacking trip through the Taganay National Park, with camping near Lake Turgoyak. Later, with my Perm comrades, we went several times to the Zyuratkul National Park in the Chelyabinsk Region and the Olenyi Ruchi Natural Park in the Sverdlovsk Region.

- Tourism remains your hobby. Who and where do you work now?
- I work in the maintenance and repair department of the Kalashnikov concern in Izhevsk. A position with an ornate title - leading engineer for the organization of operation and repair of buildings and structures.

Saxophonist

- You play the saxophone. What brought you to music?
- In high school I listened to music in the style of “Ska” and “Ska-punk”. Brass instruments are often used in these styles. After some time, I fell in love with the bright sound of the trumpet. I was 19 years old when I began to learn the basics of playing the trumpet at a music school. A year later, my classmate told me that her friend was selling a saxophone. I treated this instrument with disdain, but decided to master it too. I brought a saxophone to a teacher at a music school, but he had never played it in his life, much less taught it. I downloaded literature on the Internet and began to master the difficult instrument myself. Having moved from Tchaikovsky to Izhevsk, I played in different groups. Now I perform with the jazz "Big Band of Izhevsk State Technical University" and in a duet with Anatoly Lukin. Tolya plays the harmonica and trumpet. “... and Ivan on the saxophone” is the name of our musical tandem.

- Is it difficult to play in an orchestra?
“The first time in the university orchestra was a bit difficult, but other musicians in the group supported me. I learned the parts and began to feel more confident.

Brewer

- How did you get interested in producing homemade beer?
- I once tried a craft drink and became interested in the taste - it differs from store-bought beer. I began to study and try varieties of various craft (small) breweries in Russia and abroad. After 2 years, I decided to brew my own homemade drink. By that time, my classmate Andrei Shulakov was making beer in Perm. In December 2017, I went to see him for a couple of days. Together we brewed pumpkin spice ale. I wrote down the details and photographed the process. After brewing together, we ordered equipment and raw materials for my home brewery. I brewed my first drink myself on December 17, 2017 - I started with German wheat beer. I didn’t have enough knowledge, so I made a mistake in the amount of malt. A full tank of 50 liters came out. During the cooking process, I slightly burned my neck, ear and back. The injuries didn’t upset us too much, because the beer turned out delicious. The first brew was distributed among friends and acquaintances.

- What types of beer do you brew now?
- There are two known types of fermentation of intoxicating drinks: bottom (lager) and top (ale). Large brewing companies are preparing mass-produced European lagers. Craft breweries and homebrewers (myself included) brew mostly ales. Over two years of experimentation, I brewed more than 30 different varieties: English, American, Belgian and others. Each beer has its own English name.

- How do you produce craft beer and where do you hide the barrels of the drink?
- The process of preparing the drink is year-round. There are also seasonal beers: pumpkin ale or beer with spruce shoots, for example. Crushed malt is poured into water heated to a certain temperature - the mashing stage begins. Starch from malt is converted into sugar with the help of enzymes and wort is obtained. The liquid is cleared of malt and sent to boiling. In the second stage, hops are added for bitterness and aroma. The cooled wort is then sent to barrels for fermentation. Yeast is introduced, which eats the sugar and produces alcohol and carbon dioxide. The barrels are kept at home - top fermentation (ales) require room temperature.

- And how long to wait for the finished product?
- Beer is brewed for about 8 hours, ferments in a barrel for 2-3 weeks, and matures in a bottle from 3 weeks to 3 months (sometimes up to one year or more).

- How much beer do you brew and where do you get the ingredients?
- In 2019, I produced more than 2 tons of ale at home, and brewed another 1 ton in Perm using rented equipment. Foreign ingredients used:
+ German and Belgian solo;
+ English, German, Australian and American hops;
+ French-made yeast. I like to experiment with taste, so I add unusual foods: lactose and pumpkin, pine shoots and orange zest, spices and juices, berries, prunes and fruits, cocoa beans and coffee, Thai blue tea and hibiscus flowers.

- Where do you get containers and labels from?
- I buy bottles at a glass factory in Mozhga. The logo and the first labels were drawn for me by my classmate Vasily Yuferev. I started cooking more often, and Vasya moved to Yekaterinburg - I had to invent the stickers myself. Now Vasya is back in action, delighting with new comic-style labels.

- Do you participate in festivals and exhibitions of home brewers?
- Last year I visited 3 festivals of home brewing and small breweries in Kirov, Yekaterinburg and St. Petersburg. Visitors, organizers and brewers appreciated my drinks.

- What does the family say?
- My siblings Sergei Pavel and sisters Katya and Nadya supported me right away, but my parents were skeptical at first. This is understandable: father Alexander Ivanovich worked as a teacher of Russian language and literature, mother Lyudmila Ivanovna is also a teacher. My skill grew, and with it the trust of my parents. Now they respect this craft. On major holidays, my beer is a decoration for a family feast.

- Is craft brewing developed in Udmurtia?
- In the region, things are not so active with the production of homemade intoxicating drinks. There are still not enough people in Izhevsk to form a local club of brewers and beer lovers. There are not enough festivals and meetings to exchange experiences. I know five brewers from Izhevsk.

When it's about pleasure, the results are also pleasing. At work, Ivan the Builder is appreciated by his colleagues, home-made beer is replenished with new flavors, and views of videos from Vanya the Saxophonist’s performances are growing on the Internet.
